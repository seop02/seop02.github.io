About

I am an AI/quant researcher at Qraft Technologies, currently serving in the South Korean alternative military service program (전문연구요원). My work focuses on high-frequency ETF arbitrage, at the intersection of statistical modelling, machine learning, and low-latency engineering, mostly in C++, Rust, and Python.

Before this, I focused on developing order execution algorithms based on reinforcement learning in derivatives markets.

Research Interests

I am drawn to the broad problem of finding and exploiting structure in complex systems — identifying what genuinely carries signal, modelling the dynamics that generate it, and acting on that understanding under uncertainty and non-stationarity. Feature extraction is one facet of this; so is the choice of representation, where the right basis or invariant can render an otherwise intractable problem tractable.

This thread runs through the problems I find most compelling, whether they take the form of signal discovery in markets, learning representations from data, or the structure that governs what can be computed efficiently on a quantum device.
